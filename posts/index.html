Consciousness is perhaps the most intimate mystery we experience and yet the most distant in explanation. We wake, we feel, we think, and we know that we know. To trace the origin of consciousness is not merely to study the brain, but to journey through physics, mathematics, and the deep structure of reality itself.

At its surface, consciousness appears biological. Neurons fire, networks form, and chemical signals flow. But biology alone does not answer why these processes give rise to subjective experience.
