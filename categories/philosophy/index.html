How Emotions Think About Time

Time, as measured by clocks, moves with mechanical indifference. Seconds follow seconds with perfect equality. Yet emotional time obeys no such order. It stretches, compresses, loops, freezes, and dissolves—shaped not by physics, but by feeling. Emotions do not experience time as a line; they experience it as a landscape. To understand how emotions “think” about time is to understand how human life actually unfolds from within.
